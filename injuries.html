Most Common Runner Injuries

causes

  • overuse: you increased your mileage too fast, or increased the amount of workouts too fast, or both
  • muscle imbalances/weakness: one or more of your muscles is weak

how to fix pain

  • overuse: either take some days off until the pain subsides, or scale back your exercise, making sure at the end of the day your pain doesn't get worse. When increasing mileage or speed again, do it slowly.
  • muscle imbalances/weakness: take a few days off, roll out your muscles (the muscle that is painful, or extremely tight), start strengthening that area (don't forget to stretch and roll out too, so it doesn't continue causing the pain)

where is the pain?

upper body

lower back pain

causes: weak core, weak back, weak glutes, etc

lower body

shins

shin splints aka tibial stress syndrome

many causes including but not limited to: weak calves (generally pain on side(s) of shin), weak shins, overuse, etc

knee

patellofemoral pain syndrome

causes: overuse, weak hips/thighs, etc

bottom of foot (generally the heel)

plantar fasciitis

causes: overuse, tight calves, etc

achilles

achilles tendinopathy

causes: weak calves, overuse

outer knee/hip

IT band syndrome

causes: overuse, etc
